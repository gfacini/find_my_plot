**ATLAS Internal Note**

**CAL-NO-026**

**15 July 1993**

**Comparison between the two options for ATLAS hadronic calorimetry using simulated jets**

**L. Cozzi and L. Perini**

Milano, 9/7/93

**Introduction**

This note summarizes the work done for the comparison of the simulated performances of the "2 m" and "3 m" ("full hybrid barrel" and "intermediate hybrid barrel") options for ATLAS calorimetry as it was presented to the calorimeter working group of 30 June [1] and to the panel of 1 July; the study has been concentrated on the quality of the jet reconstruction, i.e. energy resolution and fraction of events with energy too far from the nominal value giving tails in the resolution plots.

The generation, showering and analysis of the results has been performed using Pythia 5.6, Jetset 7.3, GEANT 3.1590 with FLUKA (HADR=4) showering model and DICE2 (vers. 2.00/10 SLUG vers. 1.08/10) as present in ATLAS "dev" area (DEV directory on CSF, DEVATLAS disk on CERNVM), at the third week of June. Two sets of'single jets' (SJ) and 'double jets' (DJ) (explanation in next section) have been produced : Tab. 1 show the positions in the \(\eta\), \(E_{T}\) grid at which the jets have been generated and the number of generated events for each point; note that

\(E_{T}\,=\,300\) GeV at \(\eta=1.5\) means E = 705.7 GeV. The CPU time required on CSF (HP 9000/735) is \(\sim\) 1.6 sec/GeV (energy).

Fig. 1, 2 show the layout of the two options; the jets are fully showered in the complete ATLAS setup. Tab. 2a, 2b show the datacards used for the generations in the two options (double jets); users interested in doing similar generations should use the files ATLASJ_03 and ATLASJ_04 DATACARD, setting the KINE and * TFLF cards as they need. Tab. 3 gives a list of all the tapes available on CERNVM (PROD TAPES file). All these files reside in the ATLAS "dev" area.

### Differences between'single' and 'double' jets

Using the KINE card in SLUG input it is possible to choose whether Pythia-Jetset generates SJ or DJ.

SJ are generated by a call to LU1ENT routine while DJ by a call to LU2ENT, LUROBO, LUSHOW. For SJ indipendent fragmentation is used and energy is conserved at the level of the single jet. For DJ string fragmentation is used and energy is conserved at the level of the jet pair. Fig. 3a, 3b show the sum of the energies of the long lived particles (except \(\nu,\mu\)) present in the GEANT KINE banks for SJ, DJ (d-flavoured) of \(E_{T}=75\) GeV \(\eta=0.4\), after a cut \(\mid\eta\mid<3\) and selecting the \(\phi\) emisphere centered around the parton direction; the difference between the two kinds of jets is evident. Important difference are also present in the particle multiplicity (Fig. 4a, 4b) and thus obviously in the energy distribution of the particles and in the size of the cone needed to contain the jet fragments (Fig. 5 from [2]). Previous studies have shown that DJ are in very good agreement with the two jets events generated by full Pythia (e.g. Fig. 6 for cone size) and full Pythia is know to agree well with real dijet data (e.g. Fig. 7 from [2] showing CDF data).

In conclusion DJ are more realistic and in particular have to be used when the size of the cone used for jet reconstruction is a relevant parameter. However they are more difficult to treat (ambiguity in defining nominal energy); as the most urgent purpose of this study was to assess the comparative quality of the jet reconstruction for the two options, we have used also the SJ that should be unbiased in this respect. The energy of each jet is reconstructed using a calibration constant for each type of calorimeter/preshower (different constants for barrel and end cap) giving 7 constants for "2 m" and 8 constants for "3 m". The constants are determined separately for each (\(\eta\), E) point with a least-squares minimization method. The quantity minimized is \(\Sigma\) (\(E_{calib}~{}-~{}E_{nominal}\))\({}^{2}\) for SJ and \(\Sigma(E_{calib}-E_{KINE})^{2}\) for DJ where \(E_{KINE}\) is the sum of the energy of long lived particles present in the GEANT KINE bank after the acceptance cut used for the jet reconstruction. In most of this study the cuts used for DJ are \(\mid\eta\mid<3\) and \(0<\phi<\pi\) (partons are generated at \(\phi=\pi/2\)); some results are also given for an acceptance cut corresponding to a cone \(\Delta R=\sqrt{(\Delta\eta)^{2}+(\Delta\phi)^{2}}=0.7\)

\[\Delta\eta=\eta_{i}-\eta_{0}~{},~{}\Delta\phi=\phi_{i}-\phi_{0}\]

where \(\eta_{0},\phi_{0}\) are nominal parton directions and \(\eta_{i},\phi_{i}\) are \(\eta,\phi\) of the particles at the KINE level, and of the hits after showering and reconstruction.

## Results

### \(\eta=0.4\)

Tab. 4,5 summarize the results for SJ at \(\eta=0.4\) in the "2 m" and "3 m". Here and in the following similar tables \(<.>\) indicates the mean value of the ratio \(E_{reconstructed}/E_{nominal}\) and "out \(3\sigma^{n}\) the fraction of events having reconstructed energy different from the nominal one by more than \(3\,\times\,\sigma(E)\). In Tab. 4 the effect of reconstructing the energy with more calibration constants (13 instead of 8, i.e. 1 constant/readout layer) is reported : the amelioration in \(\sigma(E)/E\) is 10-20%. Fig. 9 shows the corresponding \(\sigma(E)/E\) distributions : note that almost none of the few events in the distribution tails have a discrepancy of \(>20\%\) in the energy reconstruction.

The performances of the two calorimeter options are similar at this level : remember that for jets only \(\sim 30\%\) of the reconstructed energy (mean value) is found in the hadronic calorimeter. Tab. 6 show the results of the linear fits of \(\sigma(E)/E\) in the form \(c_{1}/\sqrt{E}+c_{0}\); the first value reported for the "3 m" refers to the energy reconstruction with 13 constants : \(c_{0}\) is somewhat lower but the effects is hardly significant; this kind of reconstruction has not been used in the following.

Note that in this sample almost no energy is deposited in the End Caps and thus the calibration constant relevant for energy reconstruction are only 3 for the "2 m" and 4 for the "3 m". Althought the reconstruction is performed independently at the different energy the variation of the constants between the lower and higher energy point is less than 5-10% for the hadronic and electromagnetic calorimeter while reaches \(\sim 20\%\) for the preshower.

Tab. 7,8 show the same result as Tab. 4,5 but for DJ at \(\eta=0.4\) : the relevant presence of tails was carefully investigated using a specific sample and traced back to the presence of \(\Lambda^{0},K^{0}\) and their antiparticles that where decayed in JETSET, with secondary vertices very far from the primary one, before being passed to GEANT. So this is not a physical problem and has to be cured setting such particles "stable" in the Pythia datacards. On the specific 500 event sample that was investigated, only 2 events with energy loss \(>20\%\) were left after this cure and they were associated to energetic \(\pi^{+}\) depositing only a small fraction of their energy. This effect has still to be studied and understood in detail before aiming at rejection \(>1000/1\) against badly reconstructed jet as required for \(H\to l^{+}l^{-}\nu\nu\)[3].

At this stage of the work only the SJ have been then used for the evaluation of the tails in the energy resolution.

Tab. 9,10 show the \(\sigma(E)/E\) for DJ at \(\eta=0.4\) reconstructing the energy in a cone of \(\Delta R=0.7\) as explained at the end of the second section of this note, and Tab. 11 the results of the linear fits to the \(\sigma(E)/E\) values. The performances of the options are again similar and the effect of the cone is clearly seen as an increase of the sampling term.

\(\eta\) **in the crack region**

In these region (\(\eta=1.2,\eta=1.5\)) only the results of the SJ are presented as the energy resolution tails are here the most relevant issue. Note that the thicknesses of the walls of the "chimney" for the cables have been carefully simulated, but the chimney is filled with air and not with real cables.

Tab. 12,13,14,15 show the results for the two options at \(\eta=1.2\) and \(\eta=1.5\) respectively and Fig. 10, 11 show the corresponding \(\sigma(E)/E\) distributions for \(\eta=1.2\); Tab. 16 summarizes the result of the linear fits to \(\sigma(E)/E\) : the strangely low constant term at \(\eta=1.2\) may perhaps be due to the larger extent of electromagnetic calorimeter crossed at this \(\eta\) value.

Although it is difficult to draw conclusion valid at the 1% level on the energy resolution tails (as physics could need, [3]), it seems that the two calorimeter options are rather similar.

## Appendix : \(\pi^{+}\) in the ATLAS "2 m" and "3 m" setups

Positive pions of \(E_{T}=20,40,75,150\) GeV (500 events/point) were generated at \(\eta=0.4\) in both options. Tab. 17,18 and Fig. 12, 13 present the usual \(\sigma(E)/E\) information and Tab. 19 summarizes the results of the linear fits to \(\sigma(E)/E\).

Here have to be noticed two points that need carefull investigation :

* presence of significant "tails" of badly reconstructed energy (same effects as the \(\pi^{+}\) inspected in the DJ sample?)
* a sampling term by far too low respect to the results of the RD3 test beams that were declared to be compatible with the GEANT MC used in the test beam simulations [4], also when based on the same showering model (Fluka) employed in all the simulation discussed in this note.
