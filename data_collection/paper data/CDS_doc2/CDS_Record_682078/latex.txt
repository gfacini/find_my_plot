## 1 Mechanical Analysis - Dead Load With the Interior Cone Shaped Wall

### Load

The structure is loaded its self-weight. A vertical - 9,81 m/s\({}^{2}\) gravity acceleration is applied.

To take in consideration the kaptons mass which are not modelized, density of kaptons is added to density of stainless steel. The new density of stainless steel is calculated by:

\[\rho_{\mathit{new}}=\rho_{\mathit{old}}\times\frac{mass_{\mathit{st}\,\mathit{ rel}}+\,mass_{\mathit{k}\,\mathit{apton}}}{mass_{\mathit{st}\,\mathit{ rel}}}\]

\[\rho_{\mathit{new}}=7.9\times\frac{0.801+0.22}{0.801}=10.1\mathit{tonne}/\,m^{3}\]

#### 1.2.4 Normal force \(Nx\) in longitudinal bars

Longitudinal bar forces are always given in the local axis of the beam elements. The traction/compression force is oriented along the local x axis. The orientation of this axis is given by the 2 ended points limiting the beam, from point 1 to point 2. In the finite element model, all the beam have the same orientation, the node 1 is nearest the beam axis.

The plots below correspond to the reaction at extremities of bars, so a positif longitudinal force \(Nx\) (internal force) on the node 1 is **traction**_(external force)_, a negatif longitudinal force \(Nx\) on the node 1 is **compression**.

A positif longitudinal force \(Nx\) on the node 2 is **compression**, a negatif longitudinal force \(Nx\) on the node 1 is **traction**.

The plot \(9\) shows that the inner longitudinal front bar is compressed at top position. The plot _10_ shows that the force \(Nx\) is negative at node 1. The external force is traction, the plot corresponds at internal force.

### Thermal contraction

Partant de la temperature ambiante, la contraction thermiques'exprime par la relation: \(\Delta L=\alpha_{0}L_{0}\,\Delta T\)

La dimension a froid vaut alors: \(L=L_{0}+\Delta T\)

Un rechauffement delastructure permet de retrover la dimension originale, ce rechauffement s'ecrit:\(\Delta L=\alpha L\Delta T=\alpha_{0}L_{0}\Delta T\)

Nous en deduisons:

\[\alpha=\alpha_{0}\frac{L_{0}}{L}=\alpha_{0}\frac{L_{0}}{L_{0}+\Delta L}\]

La valeur du coefficient de dilatation thermique au rechauffement s'ecrit alors:

\[\alpha=\frac{\alpha_{0}}{1+\alpha_{0}\Delta T}\]

Thermal effect

The thermo-mechanical gap fluctuation is the superposition the thermal effect and of the mechanical (gravity diminished by Archimede's thrust) effect.

The thermal gap fluctuation is calculated with the formula below:

\[D=2\,R\,{\rm sin}\,\frac{B}{2}\]

\[\Delta D=D-D^{*}=2\,{\rm sin}\,\frac{B}{2}\,(R-R^{*})\]

\[R-R^{*}=\Delta\,R=\alpha R\,\Delta T\]

\[\Delta D=2\,{\rm sin}\,\frac{B}{2}\,R\,\alpha\Delta T\]
