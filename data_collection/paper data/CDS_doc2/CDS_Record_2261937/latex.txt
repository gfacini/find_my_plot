## 1 Introduction

The production of electroweak vector bosons (\(W\) and \(Z/\gamma^{*}\), which will be indicated in the remainder of this document as \(V\)) is a very important process at the LHC. Measurements of such processes allow to gain a deeper understanding of pQCD and to test the currently available predictions. These analyses are generally carried out looking at final states containing electrons or muons, exploiting relatively clean experimental signatures. With the higher centre-of-mass energy as well as the increased luminosity expected for the LHC Run 2, rarer processes like the electroweak production of vector bosons in association with two jets, including vector boson fusion processes, become more accessible as well.

Furthermore, \(V+\mathrm{jets}\) production is also a major background to a large number of analyses, such as top-quark, Higgs-boson measurements, and searches for new physics phenomena. An accurate modelling of the data is therefore necessary in this type of analyses, and often an accuracy beyond the calculated order is desired.

Typical Monte Carlo (MC) generators that are used to generate \(V+\mathrm{jets}\) events combine multi-leg matrix elements at LO [1, 2, 3] or even NLO [4] with the parton shower in order to include additional parton emissions at matrix-element accuracy. Merging algorithms are used to avoid double counting the final-state configurations that are also produced by the parton shower. It is clear that the production of reliable and high-statistics Monte Carlo (MC) generator samples is a key ingredient at multi-purpose hadron collider experiments. In the following sections, the main generators used in the ATLAS experiment [5] during Run 2 will be discussed and studied in detail.

In this note, comparisons of different generator predictions for \(V+\mathrm{jets}\) processes are made at centre-of-mass energies of 8 and \(13\,\mathrm{Te\kern-1.0ptV}\), including comparisons to recent ATLAS measurements. The studies presented follow naturally from the ones presented in [6], which focussed on comparisons in regions of phase space used mainly in Run 1 analyses. For this note, comparisons are made in regions of phase space more suitable for Run 2 analyses, with a particular focus on the production of vector bosons in association with heavy-flavour jets (also in the context of the search for Higgs decays to a \(b\)-quark pair in the same final state) and a study of systematic uncertainties for electroweak \(Vjj\) production is also performed. The scope of this note is twofold; the aim is to document the baseline samples used by ATLAS analyses in Run 2, whilst also providing insight into the differences between these samples in regions of phase space relevant to the LHC analyses, including the size of systematic uncertainties on the theoretical modelling. In addition, preliminary studies on a specific MG5_aMC+Pv8 CKKW-L tune, with rapidity-ordering shower are compared to simpler Pythia8-only tunes.

Throughout this note, \(V+\mathrm{jets}\) predictions are only shown for the \(Z/\gamma^{*}\to e^{+}e^{-}\) and \(W^{\pm}\to e^{\pm}\nu\) channel (with the exception of the study dedicated to the \(VH(H\to b\bar{b})\) background studies in Section 5.3) and the ATLAS coordinate system is adopted, which consists of a right-handed system with the \(z\)-direction defined by the beam direction, and \(x\) pointing towards the centre of the LHC ring. The polar angle \(\theta\) is defined relative to the \(z\) axis, and pseudorapidity \(\eta=-\mathrm{ln}(\tan(\theta/2))\), with the transverse direction corresponding to \(\theta=\pi/2\), \(\eta=0\). Transverse momentum, \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\), is defined as the component of momentum in the transverse direction. The polar angle, \(\phi\), is defined in the \(x-y\) plane. Angular separation is defined by \(\Delta R=\sqrt{\Delta\phi^{2}+\Delta\eta^{2}}\). Rapidity is defined by \(y=0.5\times(E+p_{z})/(E-p_{z})\), where \(E\) is the total energy, and \(p_{z}\) the component of the momentum in the \(z\) direction.

## 2 Baseline generators

In the following paragraphs, the setup of the samples used in this note for the different set of comparisons is described. Sherpa and MG5_aMC@NLO are the two most commonly used \(V+\mathrm{jets}\) generators for ATLAS Run 2 analyses using the 2015 and 2016 datasets. The focus is towards the usage of modern generator predictions, in particular the development of a NLO-merged setups.

### Sherpa

Sherpa[7] is a parton shower Monte Carlo generator simulating additional hard parton emissions [8] that are matched to a parton shower based on Catani-Seymour subtraction terms [9]. The merging of multi-parton matrix elements with the parton shower is achieved using an improved CKKW matching procedure [1, 2], which is extended to next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy using the MEPS@NLO prescription [4]. Virtual corrections for matrix elements at NLO accuracy are provided by OpenLoops[10] using the Collier tensor integral reduction library [11]. Sherpa v2.2 is used as a baseline using parton density functions (PDFs) provided by the NNPDF3.0nnlo set [12] as well as the dedicated set of tuned parton-shower parameters developed by the Sherpa authors for this version.

Matrix elements for the \(\ell^{+}\ell^{-}+\mathrm{jets}\) and \(\ell\nu+\mathrm{jets}\) final states (with \(\ell=e,\mu,\tau\)) have been generated using Sherpa v2.2 with up to two additional parton emissions at NLO accuracy and up to four additional parton emissions at LO accuracy. The matrix elements are merged with the Sherpa parton shower using the MEPS@NLO prescription using the five-flavour-number scheme (5FNS) with a matching scale of \(20\,\mathrm{GeV}\). The invariant mass of the dilepton system for the \(\ell^{+}\ell^{-}+\mathrm{jets}\) final states is required to be at least \(40\,\mathrm{GeV}\).

Each final state is sliced in the variable \(\max\left[H_{\mathrm{T}},p_{\mathrm{T}}(V)\right]\) at the parton level, where the boson transverse momentum, \(p_{\mathrm{T}}(V)\), is worked out from the two parton-level leptons and the parton-level \(H_{\mathrm{T}}\) is given by the scalar \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) sum of all parton-level jets with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>20\,\mathrm{GeV}\). Here, parton-level jets are constructed from all remaining matrix-element-level partons (excluding the two leptons) using the anti-\(k_{\mathrm{t}}\) algorithm [13] with a jet-radius parameter 0.4. Below \(\max\left[H_{\mathrm{T}},p_{\mathrm{T}}(V)\right]500\,\mathrm{GeV}\), the slices are also filtered according to their \(b\)-hadron and \(c\)-hadron content at the particle level.

Separate samples are generated for electroweak \(Vjj\) production, with two additional electroweak couplings at tree level. Matrix elements for the \(\ell^{+}\ell^{-}jj\) and \(\ell\nu jj\) final states have been generated using Sherpa v2.2 with up to two additional parton emissions at LO accuracy beyond the first two jets. At tree level, this gives rise to a gauge-invariant set of seven \(Vjj\) diagrams that involve a \(t\)-channel exchange of a heavy gauge boson using three electroweak couplings (and an additional one for the decay of the boson). The matrix elements are merged with the Sherpa parton shower using the MEPS@LO prescription with a matching scale of \(15\,\mathrm{GeV}\). The invariant mass of the dilepton system for the \(\ell^{+}\ell^{-}jj\) final states is required to be at least \(40\,\mathrm{GeV}\).

Contributions from top-quark interactions are avoided by generating matrix elements without bottom quarks for the hard-scattering process. The Min_N_TChannels option has been activated in order to remove contributions from diboson interactions that do not contain a \(t\)-channel exchange of a heavy gauge boson. The \(Vjj\) samples are produced using the \(G_{\mu}\) scheme, making use of the tree-level relations between the electroweak input parameters.

Although not explicitly shown in this note, samples for single photon production in association with jets are produced using the Sherpa v2.2 generator. Matrix elements are calculated for up to two jets at NLO accuracy and up to four jets at LO accuracy and merged with the Sherpa parton shower using the MEPS@NLO prescription. The merging scale has been set up dynamically in the scheme of [14] using \(\tilde{Q}_{\mathrm{cut}}=20\,\mathrm{GeV}\). Further phase space cuts are applied at the parton level to require \(p_{\mathrm{T}}^{\gamma}>70\,\mathrm{GeV}\) and a smooth hadronic isolation [15] of the photon with \(n=2\), \(\epsilon=0.1\), \(\delta=0.1\). The QCD factorisation and renormalisation scales were set using the STRICT_METS scale setter with a core scale of \(\mu=p_{\perp}^{\gamma}\), while \(\alpha_{\mathrm{QED}}\) is evaluated in the Thomson limit.

All Sherpa 2.2 samples mentioned above have been generated with additional weights for the NNPDF3.0nnlo replicas, \(\alpha_{\mathrm{S}}\) variations of \(\pm 0.002\) around the nominal value of 0.118, as well as 7-point scale variations of the factorisation and renormalisation scales.

### MadGraph5_aMC@NLO using CKKW-L

Matrix elements for \(V\)+ up to 4 partons at LO accuracy are produced using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO v2.2.2 [16] interfaced with Pythia v8.186 [17] for the modelling of the parton shower and underlying event. The CKKW-L matching and merging procedure is applied, with a merging scale of 30 GeV. This setup is hereafter referred to as MG5_aMC@NLO CKKW-L. The renormalisation and factorisation scales are set to the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO default values, based on a clustering of the event. The NNPDF3.0nlo PDF set is used, with \(\alpha_{S}=0.118\). The A14 tune [18] of Pythia 8 is used, and the EvtGen v1.2.0 program [19] is used for properties of the bottom and charm hadron decays. MadGraph5_aMC@NLO performs a 5-flavour (SFNS) calculation with massless \(b\)- and \(c\)-quarks in the matrix element, and massive quarks in the Pythia 8 shower. Samples produced with the described setup, sometimes referred as MG5_aMC+Py8 CKKW-L B in agreement with the definition of [6], corresponds to the main MG5_aMC+Py8 CKKW-L samples used for the studies reported in this note. A second set of samples, which use instead the NNPDF2.3lo PDF set [20] with \(\alpha_{S}=0.13\), is also available and referred as MG5_aMC+Py8 CKKW-L A, in agreement with the definition of [6].

### MadGraph5_aMC@NLO using FxFx

Samples have also been generated using the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO program to generate matrix elements for \(V\) + 0, 1 and 2 partons to NLO accuracy. The showering and subsequent hadronisation has been performed using Pythia 8.210 with the A14 tune, using the NNPDF2.3lo PDF set with \(\alpha_{s}=0.13\). The different jet multiplicities are merged using the FxFx prescription [21] implemented in the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO program (version 2.3.3 is used here).

MG5_aMC@NLO performs a 5FNS calculation with massless \(b\)- and \(c\)-quarks in the matrix element, and massive quarks in the Pythia8 shower. The PDF input set in use for event generation is NNPDF2.3nlo PDF set with \(\alpha_{\mathrm{S}}=0.119\) and the samples have been generated with additional weights for the PDF replicas as well as scale variations of the renormalisation and the factorisation scales [22].

The impact of various merging scales (\(\mu_{Q}\)) has been studied, analysing three different values: 20 GeV (down variation), 25 GeV (nominal value) and 50 GeV (up variation). At the event-generation level, the minimum jet transverse momentum, \(p_{\mathrm{T}}^{J}\) is required to be at least 8 GeV with no restriction on the absolute value of the jet pseudorapity (jet \(|\eta|\)). The samples have been generated using LHAPDF-6.1.5 [23] and FastJet-3.1.0 [24].

### Powheg MiNLO

Predictions from Powheg MiNLO [25, 26, 27] interfaced to Pythia 8.210 [17] with the AZNLO tune [28] were obtained to produce \(V+\text{jets}\) events, and will be referred to in the remainder of the note as Powheg MiNLO+Pythia8. The EvtGen v1.2.0 program [19] is used as an afterburner to better simulate the decays of bottom and charm hadrons. The Photos++ 3.61 program [29, 30] is interfaced to Pythia 8 to accurately decribe the QED final state radiation, and the emission of lepton pairs is activated in this setup. The PDF set used in Powheg is CT14nnlo [31] whereas the PDF set used in the parton shower is the CTEQ6 L1 [32] leading order set.

MiNLO is an improvement of the CKKW matching procedure to reach NLO accuracy for boson production in association one jet, and smoothly merges to NLO single boson production at low boson \(p_{\text{T}}\), using no external merging scale. NLO contributions from the CKKW Sudakov form factors are subtracted to the virtual corrections provided by PowhegBox. Thanks to the optimal scale and to the Sudakov form factors, the Powheg MiNLO samples do not need any Born suppression cut, and their cross-sections are well-behaved at small boson \(p_{\text{T}}\), contrary to fixed order predictions that diverge in this kinematic phase space.

### Alpgen

Predictions for \(V+\text{jets}\) production at 13 TeV have also been produced using the LO matrix-element generator ALPGEN v2.14 [33] interfaced to Pythia v6.426 [34] to model the parton shower, hereafter referred to as ALPGEN+Pythia6. The parton-shower tune Perugia2011C [35] and the CTEQ6L1 PDF set [32] have been used. Up to five additional partons are modelled by the matrix elements, merged with the MLM prescription [36] with a matching scale of 20 GeV. Different V plus \(N\)-partons multiplicities, with \(N\) ranging from 0 to 5, are generated in separate samples and, as no distinction is made about the flavour of the parton used in the calculation, the samples are sometimes referred to as "inclusive".

The predictions follow a four-flavour-number-scheme (4FNS) for the production of heavy-flavour jets. Explicit matrix element evaluation, with massive treatment of the heavy-flavour quarks, is also possible for the following processes (dubbed \(V+\text{hf}\)):

\[Z+b\bar{b},Z+c\bar{c},\,W+c,\,W+c\bar{c},\,W+b\bar{b}, \tag{1}\]

and, again, a total of up to five partons (generic plus heavy-flavour) can be considered in the calculation.

Each of the \(V+\text{hf}\) sample has been produced separately, with up to five partons, and a heavy-flavour overlap removal (HFOR) procedure is used combine the "inclusive" and \(V+\text{hf}\) samples. The HFOR is needed to avoid double counting of phase space between different samples because heavy-flavour quarks are both produced by the gluon splitting in the parton shower and by the explicit matrix-element calculations. The HFOR criteria require the removal of an event if it contains two heavy-flavour quarks arising from the PS with \(\Delta R(q,\bar{q})>0.4\) as well as events which contain two heavy-flavour quarks arising from ME and with \(\Delta R(q,\bar{q})<0.4\). The pairing of quarks is performed only between same flavours (\(b\) or \(c\)) and between particle/anti-particle pairs. Quarks produced by multi-parton interactions or originating directly from PDFs are not included in the HFOR procedure. The HFOR procedure reject from about 5% to 30% of the generated events, depending if the analysed sample has lower or larger parton multiplicity or on the considered parton flavour.

The HFOR procedure is applied analysing the HepMC record of each event during a pre-processing stage, before the evaluation of any other kinematic requirement. The predicted cross section of each separate sample is then scaled down to take into account the removed fraction of events. The total cross section predicted by the combination of all the samples is in agreement within 1% with the prediction of the "inclusive" only samples. Several kinematic variables are affected by the combination of the "inclusive" and \(V+\mathrm{hf}\) samples, however a net effect immediately visible is an enhancement of the differential cross section for jet multiplicities \(\geq 1\).

## 3 Rivet routines for comparison

Particle-level comparisons of different generator setups are made using Rivet v2.5.3 [38] using the following analyses:

* ATLAS 13 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) analysis of \(Z+\) jets production using an integrated luminosity of \(3.16\,\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\)[39] (not yet published in Rivet). A pair of opposite-charge dressed leptons, with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>25\)\(\mathrm{\,Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\) and \(|\eta|<2.5\) are required, with dilepton mass between 66 and \(116\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\). Jets are constructed using the anti-\(k_{\mathrm{t}}\) algorithm [13] using a jet-radius parameter of 0.4, and required to have \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>30\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\), \(|y|<2.5\) and not to overlap with the selected leptons (\(\Delta R(j,\ell)>0.4\)).
* ATLAS 8 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) measurement of the splitting scales (ATLAS_2017_I1589844) occurring in the \(k_{\mathrm{t}}\) algorithm [40; 41] using \(20.2\,\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\)[42]. In the electron channel, exactly two dressed electrons are selected with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>25\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\)and \(|\eta|<2.47\), excluding the transition region between the ATLAS barrel and end cap electromagnetic calorimeters (\(1.37<|\eta|<1.52\)). The dilepton invariant mass is required to be within \(71\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}<m_{\ell\ell}<111\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\). All charged final-state particles with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>400\,\mathrm{Me\kern-1.0ptV}\)and \(|\eta|<2.5\), excluding the tracks of the two selected electrons, are then passed through the \(k_{\mathrm{t}}\) algorithm and its splitting scales are measured for jet-radius parameters at \(R=0.4\) as well as \(R=1.0\).
* Analysis of \(V+b(b)\) events in different phase space regions: an _inclusive_ region, aimed at the differential measurement of \(W\) and \(Z\) production in association with heavy-flavour jets, a _high-\(p_{T}\) selection_, aimed at understanding this type of events when the two \(b\)-jets are almost collinear and have a very high boost as well as an _intermediate_ region, aimed at describing the main irreducible background of the \(VH(H\to b\bar{b})\) process. Jets originating from \(b\)-quarks are identified by the presence of a weakly decaying \(b\)-hadron with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>5\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\) and decay products within the area of the jet (ghost-matching). This procedure mimics a \(b\)-tagging algorithm with efficiency of 100%, therefore such jet selection is often referred as \(b\)-tagging in the following. Further details about the mentioned analyses will be given in the relevant comparison sections.
* Analysis of electroweak \(Wjj\) production, based on the ATLAS 8 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) measurement [43]. One lepton with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>25\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\) and \(|\eta|<2.5\) is required, together with a missing energy larger than \(20\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\). The \(m_{\mathrm{T}}\) is required to be larger than \(40\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\)and the two associated jets to have a transverse momentum of 80 and 60 \(\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\), respectively for the leading and subleading one, and to be within 4.4 in rapidity. Additional topological cuts are applied to the jet pair, requiring their invariant mass (\(m_{jj}\)) to be larger than \(500\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\), and \(\Delta y_{jj}>2\) and \(\Delta R_{j\ell}>0.3\).
* ATLAS 8 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) measurement of electroweak dijet production in association with a \(Z\) boson (\(Zjj\)) [44]. The corresponding routine (ATLAS_2014_I1279489) has been modified and is used to compare the 13 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) samples (without data). The selection remains the same, but the binning has been adjusted to suit the reach of the 13 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) predictions.
* Analysis of \(W+\) jets final states in the \(W\to e\nu\) channel. This routine was initially designed for a centre-of-mass energy of 8, but is used in the context of this note to study the 13 \(\mathrm{\,Te\kern-1.0ptV}\) predictions. An electron is selected and needs to be within \(|\eta|<2.47\) and have \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>25\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\). A symmetric cut on the missing energy at 25 is applied, and the transverse mass is required to be above \(40\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\). Only jets with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}>30\,\mathrm{Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\) and \(|y|<4.4\) are considered in the analysis.

## 5 \(W\) and \(Z\) boson plus heavy-flavour jets

The \(V+b(b)\) production processes are an extremely interesting benchmark for MC simulations because the predicted cross-section rates and distributions give an insight on both the structure of the proton and on the gluon splitting into a pair of \(b\)-quarks. The \(V+b(b)\) processes are also one of the largest and most difficult to model backgrounds for searches at hadron colliders. This is reflected particularly in the case where the \(p_{\mathrm{T}}(V)\) is very large, and in the case of the search for the SM \(VH(H\to b\bar{b})\) process, where they constitute the main irreducible background, and the uncertainty in their predictions largely affects the result. These aspects will be treated in the following, starting by looking at important kinematic variables for the \(V+b(b)\) process measurements, then studying the modelling in case of a large boost of the di-\(b\)-jet system, and subsequently in the context of a SM \(VH(H\to b\bar{b})\) analysis, where it is often hard to identify in data a control region pure enough in \(V+b(b)\) production, making it necessary to carefully understand the available predictions.

In this section the V+jets generators described in Section 2 are compared, at a centre-of-mass energy of 13, using custom Rivet routines aimed at selecting final states with \(b\)-jets, specifically:

* \(W(\rightarrow\ell\nu)+b(b)\)
* \(Z(\rightarrow\ell\ell)+b(b)\)
* \(Z(\rightarrow\nu\nu)+b(b)\).

### Relevant variables for the \(VH(H\to b\bar{b})\) analysis

As mentioned above, the \(V+b(b)\) process is one of the main irreducible backgrounds for in the \(VH(H\to b\bar{b})\) search. Moreover, in such a search it is often difficult to identify a pure enough control region where constrains or information on this background process could be extracted. ## 7 Tuning Pythia8 parameters in matched setups with MG5_aMC+Py8 CKKW-L

### Motivation

The Pythia8 A14 tune used in ATLAS had the parameter SpaceShower:rapidityorder turned on. This forces the initial state radiations (ISR), after the first, to be ordered in rapidity. However, when showering events generated by MG5_aMC+Px8 CKKW-L, this setting results in non-negligible dependence on the merging scale [48]. So it was decided to start from the A14 tune, and after turning this particular setting off, re-tune Pythia8 parameters in \(Z\)-boson+jets events. The A14 tune was performed for four different PDFs, but in ATLAS the version with NNPDF2.3LO is used, and in this note, A14 refers to this particular variant only.

### Strategies

1. In the first approach, referred to as Tune (RO), in MG5_aMC+Py8 CKKW-L, \(Z\)-boson events with up to 3 extra jets were generated, and showered with Pythia8 A14 tune. Then the tuning was performed on the complete matched setup, _i.e._ on the output of Pythia8 interfaced with MG5_aMC+Px8 CKKW-L.
2. In the second approach, referred to as PS-only tune (RO), it was decided to initially decouple the two generators. The tuning of Pythia8 was done separately using only Pythia8 output. Then the obtained tunes were used to shower \(Z\)-boson events with up to 3 extra jets generated in MG5_aMC+Px8 CKKW-L, and the consistency with initial Pythia-eight only tune predictions were checked.

In both cases the Rivet analysis toolkit and the Professor MC tuning system have been used, at versions 2.5.3 and 2.1.4 respectively. The Pythia version used was 8.219, with the PDFs taken from LHAPDF version 6.1.3. The MG5_aMC+Px8 CKKW-L parameters used were: Merging:Dparameter=0.2, Merging:unorderedAScalePrescrip=0, and Merging:TMS=30.

In ATLAS, tunes are performed using the Professor automated tuning tool, where each bin of each observable is parametrised as a \(N\)-dimensional 3rd order polynomial based on the sampled tune points in the parameter hypercube (\(N\) being the number of tuned parameters). These parametrisations are then used to calculate a \(\chi^{2}\) with respect to the reference data, which was numerically minimised in the parameter space to find the best tune point. Weight factors are used in the \(\chi^{2}\) and degree of freedom, \(N_{\mathrm{df}}\), calculation to place increased emphasis on certain observables.

The main focus in each case was to describe the distributions sensitive to underlying event in \(Z\)-boson events [49], so most weight was put on that. However, other analyses like measurement of event-shape in \(Z\)-boson events [50] was also looked at, and care was taken so that the \(Z\)-boson \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) distribution [51] was modelled well in each case. These three analyses were performed at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV.

In the first approach, the \(\alpha_{S}\) corresponding to ISR, FSR and MPI were set to 0.125, motivated by [52]. Then the multiple parton interaction (MPI) parameter corresponding to \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) cutoff, MultipartonInteractions:pT@Ref and the colour reconnection strength parameter ColourReconnection:range were tuned.

In the second approach, more Pythia8 parameters were tuned. They divide roughly into the initial and final state parton shower scales and couplings (SpaceShower:* and TimeShower:* parameters), and multiple parton interactions (MultipartonInteractions:* parameters). Two "beam-remnant" parameters are also tuned: the primordialKThard which generates a narrow Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum for the hard process incoming partons, and the previously mentioned ColourReconnection:range.

Traditionally, the distribution of mean \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) against charged particle multiplicity is found to be very sensitive to the modelling of the colour reconnection (CR) in underlying event analyses. The Pythia8 model for CR has recently undergone a large expansion in [53], and it was decided to test the effect of these new models as well while tuning. While all the current tunes uses the so called "MPI-based" scheme (will be referred to as CR0 model subsequently, where the ColourReconnection:range is the tunable parameter), two new models of CRs have been introduced, with corresponding tunable parameters. In the first new one, which adds more degrees of freedom to the MPI-based scheme (will be referred to as CR1 model subsequently), two parameters, ColourReconnection:m0 corresponding to the string length and ColourReconnection:junctionCorrection are tuned. It was used with ColourReconnection:allowDoubleJumRem = off setting, as recommended by the authors. In the second new model, termed the "gluon-move scheme" (will be referred to as CR2 model subsequently), three parameters were tuned: ColourReconnection:m2Lambda, ColourReconnection:fracGluon, and ColourReconnection:dLambdaCut.

A detailed description of the tuned parameters is beyond the scope of this note, and the reader is referred to the Pythia8 online documentation.

### Results and discussions

The table 3 documents the result for each of the tuning setups mentioned earlier. In each case, the parameters not mentioned were fixed at A14 value.

In Fig. 29, the performance of these tunes is shown against different ATLAS measurements. In general, the best possible tune obtained for each setup give very similar results. The plots of UE in \(Z\)-boson events are most sensitive to MPI. The charged particle sum \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) and multiplicity density as a function of \(Z\)-boson \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) in toward region show similar trends. Just switching ISR rapidity ordering off clearly deteriorates the agreement with data considerably, necessitating this tuning effort. For mean \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) against charged particle multiplicity, none of the models predict the right shape, in spite of they having different CR models. This is in strong contrast to what happens in minimum-bias events [54]. For the thrust distribution at low \(Z\)-boson \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\), where it should be most sensitive to MPI, the trend is somewhat reversed, with new tunes doing a bit worse than the A14 with ISR rapidity ordering off. Most of the tunes, except PS-only tune (RO, CR2) do very well for \(Z\)-boson \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\), which is affected mostly by ISR and BBR intrinsic \(k_{\mathrm{T}}\). Finally, for inclusive jet multiplicity in \(Z\)-boson events most tune predictions are very similar, but they all underestimate the data.

In Fig. 30, the effect of varying the merging scale by a factor of two and half on the UE observables in \(Z\)-boson events for PS-only tune (RO, CR0) tune is shown. This confirms little or no dependence on the merging scale even for the tunes where only Pythia8 output is tuned.

* It should also be noted that in spite of trying the newer CR models, the shape of the mean \(p_{\rm T}\) against multiplicity distribution could not be improved, when other UE observables and \(p_{\rm T}\) of the \(Z\)-boson were also required to modelled well. ## 8 Conclusion

The baseline Monte Carlo simulation setup used by ATLAS to model single electroweak boson production in association with jets in proton-proton collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV in Run 2 has been described. Several Monte Carlo generator predictions are compared in regions of phase space relevant for Run 2 data analyses, but also compared to recent measurements using Run-1 or early Run-2 data that has been corrected for detector effects. Comparisons are made for regions of phase space with or without additional requirements on the heavy-flavour content in the accompanying jets, as well as electroweak \(Vjj\) production. Large discrepancies between different predictions are highlighted for processes involving heavy-flavour jets and PDF and scale uncertainties are studied for all processes. In addition, dedicated sets of tuned Pythia8 parameters in matched setups with MG5_aMC+Pv8 CKKW-L are presented and discussed.
