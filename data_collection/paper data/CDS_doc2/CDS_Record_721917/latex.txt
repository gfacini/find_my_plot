B-Physics Trigger

The ATLAS Trigger will consist of three levels

Level-1 (40 MHz \(\to\) O(20 kHz))

muons, Regions-of-Interest (RoTs) in the Calorimeters

B-physics ('classical' scenario): muon with p\({}_{\rm T}\)? 6 GeV, \(|\eta|\)! 2.4

Level-2 (O(20 kHz) \(\to\) O(1-5 kHz))

RoI-guided, running dedicated on-line algorithms

B-physics ('classical' scenario): muon confirmation, ID full scan

Event Filter (O(1-5 kHz) \(\to\) O(200 Hz))

offline algorithms, alignment and calibration data available

The B-physics trigger strategy had to be revised

changed LHC luminosity target (1 \(\to\) 2x10\({}^{33}\) cm\({}^{-2}\)s-1)

changes in detector geometry, possibly reduced detector at start-up

tight funding constraintsB-Physics Trigger **II**

* Alternatives to reduce resource requirements
* require at LVL1, in addition to single-muon trigger, a second muon, a Jet or EM RoI, reconstruct at LVL2 and EF within RoI
* re-analyse thresholds and use flexible trigger strategy
* start with a di-muon trigger for higher luminosities
* add further triggers (hadronic final states, final states with electrons and muons) in the beam-coast/for low-luminosity fills
* B-physics trigger types (always single muon at LVL1)
* di-muon trigger: additional muon at LVL1
* hadronic final states trigger: RoI-guided reconstruction in ID at LVL2, RoI from LVL1 Jet trigger
* electron-muon final states trigger: RoI-guided reconstruction in TRT at LVL2, RoI from LVL1 EM trigger
* 'classical' scenario as fall-back
* Results are promising (but further studies necessary)

## B-Physics Trigger IV

* hadronic and electron-muon final states triggers
* require low-\(\mathrm{E_{T}}\) (Jet or EM) RoI from LVL1, together with a single muon; reconstruct tracks at LVL2 in RoI only
* results from detailed fast simulation, and partly from full simulation
* bunch-crossing identification not yet implemented in full simulation
* hadronic trigger (e.g. \(\mathrm{E_{T}}\)?5 \(\mathrm{\,Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\))
* \(\sim\)2 RoI's from fast simul.
* \(\sim\)10 RoI's from full simul. (w/o BCD)
* electron-muon trigger (e.g. \(\mathrm{E_{T}}\)?2 \(\mathrm{\,Ge\kern-1.0ptV}\))
* \(\sim\)1 RoI from fast simul.
* trigger rates (1x10\({}^{33}\) cm-\({}^{2}\)s-1); \(\sim\)200 Hz after LVL2, \(\sim\)20 Hz after EFRecent ATLAS Studies on J/\(\psi\)

The current main emphasis in lies on 'technical issues' (validating/optimising trigger and offline s/w architecture, performance, etc.), not on doing full-fledged, detailed physics analyses

* shown results are taken from a study on the performance of a staged detector in an initial period of 1 fb-1
* study on measuring the direct J/\(\psi\) production cross-section (N. Panikashvili, M. Smizanska)
* will be one of the first B-physics measurements in ATLAS
* large J/\(\psi\) rate after LVL1, direct J/\(\psi\) contribution not known
* important to find the best strategy to select b-events (e.g. interplay/optimisation of p\({}_{\mathrm{T}}\) vs. vertexing cuts)

* background studies not yet included
* generation of events used colour-octet model in PYTHIA (implemented by M. Sanchis)

\(\mathbf{B_{c}}\) studies in ATLAS

* The expected large production rates at the LHC will allow for precision measurements of \(\mathbf{B_{c}}\) properties \(\mathbf{\alpha}\)_recent estimates_ for ATLAS (assuming f(b \(\rightarrow\)\(\mathbf{B_{c}}\))\(\sim\)10-3, 20 fb-1, LVL1 muon with p\({}_{\mathrm{T}}\)? 6 _GeV_, \(|\eta|\)! 2.4) \(\bullet\)\(\sim\)5600 \(\mathbf{B_{c}}\)\(\rightarrow\)J/\(\psi\)\(\pi\) events \(\bullet\)\(\sim\)100 \(\mathbf{B_{c}}\)\(\rightarrow\)\(\mathbf{B_{s}}\)\(\pi\) events
* Channels studied so far: \(\mathbf{B_{c}}\)\(\rightarrow\)J/\(\psi\)\(\pi\) (mass measurement), \(\mathbf{B_{c}}\)\(\rightarrow\)J/\(\psi\)\(\mu\)v (clean signature, ingredient for \(|V_{\mathrm{cb}}|\) determ.)

Sanchis et al., hep-ph/9506306, hep-ph/9510450) \(\circ\)parametrised detector response (ID) _estimate_ of \(\sim\)10000 \(\mathbf{B_{c}}\)\(\rightarrow\)J/\(\psi\)\(\pi\) events \(\circ\)mass resolution \(\mathbf{\sigma_{B_{c}}}\)= 40 MeV \(\circ\)accuracy of mass measurement \(\sim\)0.5 MeV\(\mathbf{B_{c}}\) **Studies in ATLAS II**

* Recent developments (_C._ Driouichi et al., hep-ph/0309120, several notes in preparation) Since the production of \(B_{c}\) is suppressed by the hard production of an additional \(cc\) pair, also MC generation of \(B_{c}\) events using standard tools is CPU intensive.
* example: 100,000 PYTHIA pp events \(\rightarrow\) 1 \(B_{c}\) event (which does not necessarily survive the ATLAS LVL1 Trigger selection) \(\rightarrow\) **Implementation of two MC generators in PYTHIA 6.2*
