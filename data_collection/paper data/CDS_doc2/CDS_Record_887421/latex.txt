**Abstract**

_The analysis presented in this note is a comparative study of the observability potential in the lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron final state of a Higgs boson decay into \(\tau\) lepton pair. Discussed are mass points \(m_{H}\) =120GeV, 150GeV and 200GeV and production in b-quark fusion. It is based on the fast simulation of the ATLAS detector. Results are given for the SM cross-sections, the cross-section enhancement of the MSSM regarding tan\(\beta\) is not included. It is shown that introducing lepton-lepton mode to the analysis stream may increase signal significance of about 20-40%._

## 1 Introduction

The investigations of Higgs boson decay into \(\tau\) lepton pair with different Higgs production mechanisms and decay modes of taus based on the full simulation of ATLAS detector were carried out for the ATLAS Physics TDR [1]. More detailed studies, within the MSSM of direct and Yukawa induced Higgs boson production mechanisms in mass range (150-450GeV) were documented in [2, 3]. They concluded, that the MSSM Higgs boson can be discovered in lepton-hadron mode over large range of (\(m_{A}\), \(tan\beta\)) parameter space already at \(30fb^{-1}\). The contribution from lepton-lepton channel mode was concluded as negligible already in [3] and not discussed afterwards. An extension of these studies to the mass range up to 800GeV was performed for lepton-hadron channel [4] and hadron-hadron channel [5]. It was therefore very interesting to notice that in the analyses for VBF production \(qq\to qqH\) and \(H\to\tau\tau\) the lepton-lepton channel was bringing \(\sim\) 30% of an additional signal significance [6].

The aim of the study presented here was to compare lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron channel and their contribution to the total significance of \(b\overline{b}H,H\to\tau\tau\) process. The results were obtained with SM cross-sections times branching ratio and could be used as a reference point for MSSM scans, where cross-sections of Yukawa induced \(b\overline{b}H\) production is highly enhanced (\(\sim tan^{2}\beta\)).

The mass resolution and signal acceptances were discussed already in paper [7]. We concentrated here on discussing background processes. The studies of various Monte Carlo approachesto generate expected background coming from \(Z/\gamma^{*}\), W and \(t\bar{t}\) processes are an important part of the analysis. The background from associated \(Zb\bar{b}\) and \(Wb\bar{b}\) is also separately estimated. The different approaches to generate the \(t\bar{t}\) background starting from \(2\to 2\) process up to inclusive \(2\to 6\) process are also considered.

## 2 Events generation

In presented analysis interactions of heavy quarks and gauge bosons lead to very promising experimental signatures. Recently created framework AcerMC-2.0 [8] was designed for Monte Carlo simulations of such events and it implements the massive matrix elements that describe associated production of heavy quarks and Z, W bosons. The associated Higgs boson production is available as native PYTHIA process. The AcerMC is a fortran-based framework interfaced to PYTHIA 6.214 [9], Tauola [10] and Photos [11] packages. It provides framework to generate both native PYTHIA processes or native AcerMC processes. If not stated otherwise, PYTHIA matrix element were used to generate hard process events, while Tauola and Photos packages were used for correct simulation of tau-decays and radiative QED bremsstrahlung. Events generated with PYTHIA or AcerMC native processes where processed with PYTHIA parton shower model. The Data Challenge 2 (DC2) recommended parameters for pile-up events were set for PYTHIA initialization [12].

Two Higgs boson production mechanisms were considered in this analysis, see Table 1 and Table 2. As in the Standard Model gluon fusion is a dominant Higgs boson production channel, it is chosen to be the reference for Yukawa induced Higgs boson production associated with b-quark, which is of primary interest for this analysis. There are three approaches for modeling this process. The direct \(b\bar{b}\to H\) fusion with no high \(p_{T}\) b-jet in the event (b-jet veto) has the highest cross-section. However it is easier to reduce background for \(H\to\tau\tau\) decay channel requiring one identified b-jet in the event. The associated Higgs boson and b-quark production, \(gb\to b\bar{H}\) yields one high \(p_{T}\) b-jet. The third approach, \(gg\to b\bar{b}H\), is characterized by at least two b-jets in the event.

The background in our analysis comes from W, \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) and \(t\bar{t}\) production. Inclusive (\(2\to 1\)) single W production process with additional QCD jets was chosen as it has a large cross-section. It has been generated in five \(p_{T}\) windows with \(2.0\cdot 10^{6}\) events in each bin.

Another approach takes a \(2\to 2\) W + jet production process. This background was generated in five \(p_{T}\) windows as well, but since the cross-section has singularity when \(p_{T}^{W}\to 0\), the default PYTHIA threshold on minimal \(p_{T}\) of 1 GeV was applied.

The associated \(b\bar{b}W\) was generated with AcerMC matrix elements. It has 3 order of magnitude smaller cross-section times branching ratio, but has 2 b-quarks in the final state and can fake signal events easier than W + jet events.

Irreducible Drell-Yan \(Z/\gamma^{*}\to\tau\tau\) production will be a main background at the Higgs boson mass at 120 GeV. It has been generated in eight \(\mathrm{m}_{Z}\) windows with \(2.0\cdot 10^{6}\) events in each bin.

Results obtained with inclusive Drell-Yan process discussed above have been cross-checked with estimates for background from associated \(Zb\bar{b}\) production. Events were generated with AcerMC matrix elements.

The \(t\bar{t}\) background was generated with the AcerMC generator matrix elements. We considered \(2\to 6\) resonant process \(gg,q\bar{q}\to t\bar{t}\to f\bar{f}f\bar{b}\bar{b}\) and complete gg, \(q\bar{q}\to WWb\bar{b}\to f\bar{f}f\bar{f}b\bar{b}\). The off-shell \(2\to 2\) production process \(gg,q\bar{q}\to t\bar{t}\) (as implemented in PYTHIA) was also taken into account.

The fully generated events were passed to the fast simulation package of the ATLAS detector. ATLFAST-2.60 [13]. ATLFAST package: reconstructs isolated leptons and photons, labels b-jets, c-jets and \(\tau\)-jets and estimates the missing transverse energy.

The labeling of \(\tau\)-jets in the pseudorapidity range of \(|\eta|<2.5\) is based on the energy deposition profile in the calorimeter and number of tracks pointing to the calorimeter cluster. The more detailed study was performed on fully simulated events already some time ago [14] and were parametrized for the fast simulation and implemented in Atlfast-b. We have checked and shown in Appendix A in [7], that the calibration of tau-jets is not required. For signal and \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) background in the lepton-hadron channel, where true \(\tau\)-jets are present in the event, fixed efficiency of 50% was set. For the W and the \(t\overline{t}\) backgrounds the random \(p_{T}\)-dependent \(\tau\)-tagging procedure with nominal efficiency of 50% was applied.

The b-jet calibration is needed to obtain realistic values for reconstructed kinematical quantities (close to partonic ones) [15]. In case of b-jets, Atlfast labeling algorithm relies on finding within cone \(\Delta R_{cone}<0.2\) around the jet axis a b-quark after FSR with \(p_{T}>5\)GeV. The calibration factor is \(p_{T}\)-dependent and for lower \(p_{T}\) is of the order of 1.4. In this analysis the random b-jet tagging procedure with efficiency of 60% and rejection 90 against QCD jets was applied on all samples.

It is assumed that electrons and muons can be reconstructed in the pseudorapidity range of \(|\eta|<2.5\) with efficiency of 90%. Lepton2 isolation for semi-leptonic decay is rather loose, this background can be suppressed even stronger. Fake leptons (e/jet separation) are not included. All efficiencies were summarized in Table 3.

### Trigger and basic selection

In this analysis a Higgs boson decay into \(\tau\)-lepton pair is characterized by at least one lepton in the final state. This allows to filter interesting events on the base of a single or di-lepton trigger.

In case of **lepton-lepton** analysis the following selections were used:

* at least 1 isolated lepton in the event * the single lepton trigger: * \(p_{T_{e}}\geq\) 25GeV in case of 1 electron * \(p_{T_{\mu}}\geq\) 20GeV in case of 1 muon * the di-lepton trigger: * \(p_{T_{e1,e2}}\geq\) 15GeV for 2 electrons * \(p_{T_{\mu,1,\mu 2}}\geq\) 10GeV for 2 muons * \(p_{T_{e}}\geq\) 15GeV and \(p_{T_{\mu}}\geq\) 10GeV for pair of 1 \(e\) and 1 \(\mu\)
* same as Trigger selection, but exactly 2 leptons in the event are required in order to reconstruct di-_tau_ lepton pair mass.

In case of **lepton-hadron** analysis:

* 1 isolated lepton in the event with \(p_{T_{lep}}\geq\) 20GeV
* Trigger selection + additional 1 \(\tau\)-tagged jet with \(p_{T_{\tau=jet}}\geq\) 30GeV.

### Invariant mass of the \(\tau\) pair

The signal from the Higgs boson will be observed as a peak in the di-\(\tau\) invariant mass spectrum. In order to reconstruct it, one needs to resolve 4-momenta of neutrinos coming from \(\tau\)'s decays. The key assumptions in this procedure are:

* the \(\tau\) lepton is massless
* the visible product of \(\tau\) lepton decay is close to \(\tau\) direction itself (collinearity)

The solution of the transverse momenta conservation law equations for the Higgs boson decay leads to expressions for fractions of the two \(\tau\) momenta carried by visible decay products \(x_{\tau_{1}}\) and \(x_{\tau_{2}}\). The fraction values of \(x_{\tau_{i}}\) should fall in range (0,1). In this case neutrino's 4-momenta can be resolved and the invariant mass of the \(\tau\) system can be expressed as:

\[m_{\tau\tau}=m_{vis}/\sqrt{x_{\tau_{1}}\cdot x_{\tau_{2}}} \tag{1}\]

where \(m_{vis}\) is a mass of visible Higgs decay products, \(x_{\tau_{i}}\) are the fraction of momenta carried by them. In the analysis of lepton-lepton channel 2 leptons are used, while in lepton-hadron channel - 1 lepton and 1 \(\tau\)-jet.

Additional Optimization selection was also applied to suppress the background and improve mass resolution. The consecutive cuts in **lepton-lepton** case consisted of:

* Threshold on the minimal angular separation between two leptons, \(|sin(\Delta\phi_{\ell t})|>0.2\)
* \(p_{T}^{miss}>30GeV\)* Threshold on the minimal angular separation between two leptons \(cos(\Delta\phi_{\ell\ell})>-0.9\)
* Distance between 2 leptons in \((\eta,\phi)\) plane: \(\Delta R_{\ell\ell}<2.8\).

And additionally for the same flavour leptons (SF):

* Cut on invariant mass of di-lepton system \(20GeV<m_{\ell\ell}<80GeV\)
* \(p_{T}^{miss}>50GeV\)

The Optimization selection in **lepton-hadron** case consisted of:

* Threshold on the minimal angular separation between lepton and \(\tau\)-jet, \(|sin(\Delta\phi_{\ell\tau-jet})|>0.2\)
* Threshold on the maximal missing transverse mass, calculated from lepton and missing transverse energy \(m_{T}^{\ell,miss}=\sqrt{2\cdot p_{T}^{\ell}}\cdot p_{T}^{miss}\cdot(1-cos( \Delta\phi_{\ell\bar{\sigma}_{T}^{miss}}))\)
* \(p_{T}^{miss}>30GeV\)
* Threshold on the minimal angular separation between lepton and \(\tau\)-jet, \(cos(\Delta\phi_{\ell\tau-jet})>-0.9\)
* Distance between visible \(\tau\)'s decay products in \((\eta,\phi)\) plane \(\Delta R_{\ell\tau-jet}<2.8\).

Distributions of variables used for cuts are presented on Figures 1 and 2. The background distributions are normalized to total \(\sigma\times BR\), while signal is additionally scaled by a factor of \(10^{3}\) (\(10^{4}\) for \(|sin(\Delta\phi)|>0.2\) and \(cos(\Delta\phi)>-0.9\) cut) to make it visible on the figure.

The further analysis for each final mode: lepton-lepton (NSF and SF) and lepton-hadron, splits into two streams. The b-jet veto - when there is no b-jet in the event, and b-jet tag - with at least one b-jet. Details can be found in Section 6.1.

## 5 Acceptance for signal and background events

The acceptance was calculated as a ratio of number of accepted events after given cut and number of generated events \(N^{acc}/N^{gen}\). The error of acceptance3 was estimated according to binomial distribution with \(p=N^{acc}/N^{gen}\) and \(n=N^{gen}\). It should be noticed that there is no big difference in acceptance after Basic selection for various Higgs boson production mechanism and the same final signatures (ca. 22% for lepton-lepton and ca. 11% for lepton-hadron channel), see Table 4 and Table 6. Although acceptance after resolving neutrino's four-momenta is similar for all production processes (ca. 13% and 5%), after optimization selection, which leads to improvement of mass resolution, the acceptance is 3.0/1.2=2.4 (0.94/0.27=3.5) times larger (between \(gb\to bH\) and \(b\overline{b}\to H\)) in lepton-lepton (lepton-hadron) channel.

Footnote 3: In order to obtain total acceptance for background processes generated in a \(p_{T}\) or mass bins, we summed acceptances in given bin weighted with cross-sections in given bin and normalized to total cross-section for this process.

The acceptance for various backgrounds is shown on Table 5 and Table 7. The \(Zb\overline{b}\) background has the most similar topology to signal, and it has the highest acceptance after basic selection from all backgrounds in both lepton-lepton (13.5%) and lepton-hadron channel (3.44%).

In case of the **W background** an acceptance for the \(Wb\overline{b}\) process, after basic cut, is at the level of 0.16% and it is 2 orders of magnitude higher than for inclusive W or W + jet background in lepton-lepton channel. In lepton-hadron channel Wbb acceptance after basic cuts is two times higher than other W background (ca. 0.2 %). The reason for it is that a semi-leptonic decay of b-quark is more frequent than that of a hadronic jet. A misidentification of electrons is not taken into account.

The different approaches to the **t\(\overline{\mbox{t}}\) production** mechanism yield similar results in an acceptance after basic selection both in lepton-lepton (3.6%) and lepton-hadron channel (1.5%). See Table 8 and Table 9. The number of expected events for \(10fb^{-1}\) is also similar on all level of cuts, thus the full \(2\to 6\) process \(gg,q\overline{g}\to\mbox{WW}b\overline{b}\to f\overline{f}f\overline{f}b \overline{b}\), as generated with AcerMC-2.0 MC generator, was chosen for further analysis.

The consecutive cuts are rejecting various background. In lepton-lepton channel a though cut on \(|sin\Delta\phi|>0.2\) eliminates 1-1.44/2.39=40% of the signal, it reduces about 1-5.44\(\cdot 10^{4}\)/9.85\(\cdot 10^{4}\) = 65% of \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) and 1-503/706= 34% of W background in lepton-lepton and ca. 1-2.09\(\cdot 10^{4}\)/4.06\(\cdot 10^{4}\) = 50% of both in lepton-hadron channel.

The cut on missing transverse mass (\(m_{T}^{t,miss}\)) is efficient for W and \(t\overline{t}\) backgrounds reduction (ca. 1-7.06\(\cdot 10^{3}\)/1.47\(\cdot 10^{4}\)= 50%) and it is imposed only in lepton-hadron channel.

The threshold of 30 GeV set on \(p_{T}^{miss}\) cuts off both Z and W backgrounds at high rate (1-4.95\(\cdot 10^{3}\)/2.03\(\cdot 10^{4}\)= 75%) in lepton-hadron channel. The W background has insufficient statistic in lepton-lepton channel.

The additional cut on \(cos(\Delta\phi)\) restricts the angular distance between visible \(\tau\) decay products by extra 28\({}^{\circ}\) respect to \(|sin(\Delta\phi)|\) cut. It yields in ca. 1-1.24\(\cdot 10^{4}\)/1.45\(\cdot 10^{4}\)= 18% reduction of all backgrounds in both lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron channel.

## 6 Expected number of events and the mass resolution

The expected number of events is calculated as \(\it{const}\times\sigma\times\it{Acceptance}\), where const contains electron or \(\tau\)-jet identification efficiency and coefficent \(10^{4}\) coming from change from \(\it{pb}^{-1}\) to \(10fb^{-1}\). The error is estimated according to the total derivative. I assumed 10% uncertainty on \(\sigma\). The expected number of events is shown in Table 10 and Table 11 for lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron channel respectively. The efficiency and rejection factors used are described in Section 3.

After optimization selection in lepton-lepton channel the Z background and the \(\it{t\overline{t}}\) backgrounds are comparable (\(1.19\cdot 10^{4}/(1.19\cdot 10^{4}+241+1.42\cdot 10^{4})=45\%\) and \(54\%\)). The optimization procedure accepts \(1.19\cdot 10^{4}/9.85\cdot 10^{4}\)= 12% of expected \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) events in respect to the basic selection plus resolved neutrino cut. The W background is marginal (less than 1% of total) through all cuts.

In lepton-hadron channel W background becomes more important. After optimization selection it contributes \(1.78\cdot 10^{3}/(4.41\cdot 10^{3}+1.78\cdot 10^{3}+3.5\cdot 10^{3})\)= 18% of the total background. The optimization cuts accepts \(4.41\cdot 10^{3}/4.06\cdot 10^{4}\)= 11% of \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) background, \(1.78\cdot 10^{3}/5.98\cdot 10^{4}\)= 3% of the W background and \(3.5\cdot 10^{3}/1.76\cdot 10^{4}\)= 20% of the \(\it{t\overline{t}}\). Actually, every cut imposed on \(\it{t\overline{t}}\) reduces it ca. 20% on each level (except the missing transverse mass: 52%).

In Table 12 and Table 13 the mass resolution, fitted within \(\it{m_{H}}=120\it{GeV}\pm 20\it{GeV}\) window, after consecutive cuts is presented. It can be noticed that the mass resolution of Yukawa induced processes improves after optimization selection on average (\(25.8\cdot 16.6\))/\(25.8\)= 35% in lepton-lepton and (\(22.2\cdot 15.2\))/\(22.2\)= 30% in lepton-hadron case. The best resolution is obtained for the \(\it{gb}\to\it{bH}\) in both lepton-lepton (\(14.6\)GeV) and lepton-hadron (\(12.3\)GeV) channel. The mass resolution after optimization selection is comparable with our previous study [7] on level of 3% except \(\it{b\overline{b}}\to\it{H}\) and \(\it{b\overline{b}H}\) in lepton-lepton channel where we obtained ca. 15% worser resolution. It might be due to change of the cut sequence.

It should be also pointed out that in case of the lepton-hadron channel we obtain (\(16.6\)-\(13.5\))/\(16.6\)= 18% better mass resolution than in lepton-lepton one. It is reasonable and consistent with reported results from full simulation [16], since in lepton-hadron case there are three neutrinos (instead of four) that escape detection and the assumption on the collinearity of the \(\tau\) decay products with respect to \(\tau\) itself works better in this case. The reconstructed mass of \(\tau\) lepton pair with gaussian fit in range \(\it{m_{H}}=120\it{GeV}\pm 20\it{GeV}\) is shown on Figure 3.

### Analysis streams with b-jet tag and b-jet veto

In the final state of signal events apart from \(\tau\)-jets, present are also b-quarks. The b-jet tagging is an important ingredient of the analysis. For ATLAS detector, b-tagging performance was studied with full simulation. Here, we use only parametrized version, a \(p_{T}\)-dependent b-jet tagging procedure, from ATLFAST-b with nominal b-jet tagging efficiency of 60%. See section 3 for more details. The case when the "b-tagging procedure" has found at least one b-jet is called "b-jet tagged" event, the opposite case, when no b-jet has been found is called "b-jet veto" event.

The b-jet tagged analysis is efficient, to suppress by factor \(5.81\cdot 10^{3}/448\)= 13 the \(Z^{0}/\gamma^{*}\) background, in NSF and SF of lepton-lepton and in lepton-hadron analysis. The inclusive \(W^{\pm}\) is suppressed by factor 47/2.82= 17 in NSF and 24 times in SF (13 times in lepton-hadron mode). An interesting observation is that a background, that comes from associated Z and W production with b-quark behaves differently. The b-jet tagging procedure should accept more events, than b-jet veto one for processes with topology containing at least one high \(p_{T}\) b-quark. This is true for all signal and background events except \(Wb\overline{b}\).

On the other side, signal containing at least one b-jet in the final state is reduced only 1.7 times (ca. 0.106/0.181= 60%) in both lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron channel. Analogous number are obtained for the expected number of events within a mass window \(m_{H}=120GeV\pm 20GeV\).

The mass resolution slightly improves (ca. (13.5-13.3)/13.5= 1%) when applying b-jet tagged stream analysis in lepton-hadron and ca. (18.0-17.6)/18.0= 3.6% in lepton-lepton channel.

Applying b-jet veto one reduces 0.196/0.0781= 2-3 times associated Higgs boson production while the \(t\overline{t}\) background is reduced \(3.5\cdot 10^{3}/1.09\cdot 10^{3}\) = 3-4 times. For the gluon fusion we loose only (1-28.6/30.5=) 7% of the signal.

In order to suppress the \(t\overline{t}\) background even more, additional cut, as used in ATLAS Physics TDR, was set (called 3\({}^{rd}\) jet veto). It requires that there should be no more, than two non b-jets in the event with \(p_{T}>15GeV\) and \(|\eta|<3.2\).

In lepton-lepton channel the Z background becomes dominant for the b-jet veto analysis (96% for NSF and 87% for SF of total), while the \(t\overline{t}\) background becomes dominant in the b-jet tag analysis ( 949/(321+0.82+949)= 75% for NSF and 85% for SF). The W background is marginal (less than 1% of total).

In lepton-hadron channel the W background becomes more important. It contributes 818/(2.49-103+818+91.4)= 24% (b-jet veto) and 11% (b-jet tag) of the total. The Z background is dominant again in b-jet veto analysis stream, while \(t\overline{t}\) background is leading in b-jet tag analysis (60% of total).

We noticed that the mass resolution get worser after application of 3\({}^{rd}\) jet veto cut, however we gained better signal to background ratio.

### Events in the mass window

Once the mass of di-\(\tau\) system is reconstructed the number of expected events within mass window \(m_{H}=120GeV\pm 20GeV\) for an integrated luminosity \(10fb^{-1}\) can be calculated and is presented in Table 14 (lepton-lepton) and Table 15 (lepton-hadron mode).

The main background in lepton-lepton channel in b-jet veto analysis stream arise from resonant \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) and after all cuts constitutes 1130/(1130+13.3+29.9)= 95% of total background. The \(Zb\overline{b}\) process has similar topology to the signal and after all cuts contributes only 23.2/1130= 2% to possible \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) background events in b-jet veto analysis and 35.4/110= 30-40% in b-jet tagged.

The background from W is marginal in lepton-lepton channel. The background from \(t\overline{t}\) contributes 70-80% of total in b-jet tag analysis.

In lepton-hadron channel the W background comprises \(434/1.46\cdot 10^{3}+434+646=17\%\) of the total background after optimization selection. In the b-jet veto analysis the \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) is dominant ( 796/(796+205+21.3)= 68%), while in b-jet tagged analysis stream the \(t\overline{t}\) background grows to 48% of total and is comparable with the \(Z/\gamma^{*}\) background ( 79/(79+16.6+88.3)= 43%).

## 7 Sensitivity in lepton-lepton versus lepton-hadron channel

Finally, we compared lepton-lepton vs. lepton-hadron channel in terms of signal over square root of background ratio. Errors are estimated according to the total derivative. The signal significance is computed, see Table 16, by adding in quadrature significances of all components. First it adds NSF and SF for lepton-lepton channel and then it combines lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron modes. These results were obtained for the Higgs boson of the SM and do not take into account the MSSM enhancement of signal cross-section due to dependence of \(tan^{2}\beta\).

The similar calculations were performed also for mass points \(m_{H}=150GeV\) and \(m_{H}=200GeV\) with a mass window of 30GeV and 40GeV respectively. The corresponding tables for signal acceptance (Table 18 Table 19, Table 20, Table 21), number of expected events (Table 22, Table 23, Table 24, Table 25), number of expected events in a mass window (Table 30, Table 31, Table 32, Table 33) and a mass resolution (Table 26, Table 27, Table 28, Table 29) can be found in the Appendix. The signal significance for these mass points is shown in Table 17.

It turns out, that the contribution from lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron channel is comparable after optimization selection as preliminary studies presented in [7] had suggested. The "b-jet veto" analysis stream decreases signal significance of \(b\overline{b}H\) production channel as compared to generic selection. For the Higgs boson of 120GeV, the contribution of lepton-lepton mode increases 45% the combined significance. The "b-jet tag" analysis results in a raise of the signal significance. The contribution of lepton-lepton channel for the Higgs boson of 120GeV is 23% and decreases for higher masses.

## 8 Conclusions

In this note an analysis of the signal and background processes for associated Higgs boson with bottom quarks production on basis of the SM with \(\sigma\times BR\) and \(m_{H}=120GeV\), based on fast simulation of ATLAS detector, is presented.

It has been confirmed that background originating from the W boson is marginal in lepton-lepton channel. The dominant background for b-jet veto analysis stream is \(Z/\gamma^{*}\), while for b-jet tag analysis is \(t\overline{t}\).

The presented study confirms that the contribution from lepton-lepton channel is decreasing for higher masses of the Higgs boson, however it is important for low mass region where it contributes on level of 20-40% to the total significance.

The observed fact of worse acceptance of \(Wb\overline{b}\) after b-jet tagging procedure in both lepton-lepton and lepton-hadron channel should be investigated in more details. However it is not crucial for the presented here analysis, since \(Wb\overline{b}\) contributes, depending from the analysis stream and decay mode, from 0.3-6.5% of total expected W background (13.5% for NSF lepton-lepton channel).
